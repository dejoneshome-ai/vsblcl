# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners of small independent places to stay across mid Wales — B&Bs, self-catering
cottages, farm stays, guesthouses, small hotels, glamping and shepherd's huts. Usually
the owner-operator themselves, not a marketing person. Time-poor, no agency budget,
often paying 15%+ commission to Booking.com / Airbnb and frustrated by it. They browse
on a phone, decide quickly, and trust a real local person over a faceless agency.

## Product Purpose

VisibleCymru helps these businesses (1) get found online and (2) get booked direct —
keeping the commission that OTAs currently skim. A service ladder makes it approachable:
free visibility Snapshot → £75 Visibility Package → £395 Website. The Direct Booking
System is now a named product in its own right — a real-time availability calendar with
secure card payments and a mobile-first guest flow, sold as the next step after the £395
build or as a standalone add-on. Success = a mid-Wales host taking commission-free
bookings from their own site.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, warm, honest, unhurried. A real person (Dylan) who knows the patch, uses AI
openly for the heavy research, and shows the work before asking to be paid. Premium and
editorial — the confidence of good craft — but never corporate or SaaS-slick. Voice is
plain-spoken and local ("places people stay & visit", "staying in mid Wales"), not
jargon.

## Anti-references

- Generic startup/SaaS landing pages (cream backgrounds, gradient text, hero-metric stat
  blocks, identical icon-card grids, an uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section).
- Faceless agency sites with stock-smiley teams and retainer pricing.
- Anything that reads as "AI made that" — the slop tells above are banned outright.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site itself is the £395 proof — its craft is the pitch.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Interactive proof over claims: the live snapshot demo, the OTA
   calculator worked out on the visitor's own numbers, a real booking mockup.
3. **Grounded in place.** Forest-and-earth palette, Welsh hills, sheep, a bilingual
   *Croeso* — friendly and unmistakably mid-Wales. Warmth comes from colour, illustration
   and copy, not from a stock hero photo.
4. **A ladder, not a wall.** Every visitor can start free and climb only as far as they need.
5. **Honest scarcity.** Launch-partner limits reflect real capacity (a handful at a time),
   never a fake countdown.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 (verified across slate and sage surfaces), large/UI ≥3:1.
Visible keyboard focus on every interactive element, cursor affordance on all clickables,
44px touch targets (including the calculator sliders). Full `prefers-reduced-motion`
support — every reveal/animation has a static fallback and no content is gated behind a
transition. Responsive at 375 / 768 / 1024 / 1440px with no horizontal scroll.
